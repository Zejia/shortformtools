    TikTok bio notes

    A TikTok bio has to work quickly. Name the audience, give a reason to follow, and avoid vague phrases like daily content or good vibes unless they are part of a specific brand voice. The generator creates options around audience, promise, tone, and proof so you can test a few directions without losing the account's main focus.

    Use the bio after you already know what kind of videos the account publishes. If the videos are tutorials, the bio should make the learning promise obvious. If the videos are product demos, the bio should explain the buyer or use case. If the account is personal, the bio still needs a repeatable reason for a stranger to follow.

    How long should a TikTok bio be?

    Shorter is usually easier to scan. Start with one sentence, then add proof or a call to action only if it makes the account clearer.

    Should I mention my niche?

    Yes, if it helps the right viewer understand the account. A focused niche also supports stronger video hooks and hashtags.

    Bio quality check

    • The first phrase should tell the right viewer that the account is for them.
    • The promise should match the videos you can publish repeatedly, not just one viral idea.
    • The proof or CTA should be specific enough to act on, such as templates weekly, new audits every Friday, or start with the pinned post.
