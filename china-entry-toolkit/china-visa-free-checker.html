What to do next

    How to read the result

    Use the verdict like a travel planning filter, not a final legal ruling.

    If the checker says eligible

    That means your nationality, trip purpose, and stay length line up with the current unilateral visa-free framework modeled here. You should still keep lodging, return or onward travel, and trip-purpose evidence ready for airline and border checks.

    If the checker says not covered

    That does not always mean you cannot travel. It usually means one of three things: your nationality is not on the unilateral list, your purpose falls outside the covered activities, or your stay is too long for the 30-day rule.
